Mapping Air Quality in the Netherlands

A comprehensive GIS analysis of NO&dsub2; pollution, land cover change, and population exposure using satellite data and geospatial processing.

Project Overview

This project analyzes the spatial relationship between air quality (NO&dsub2; concentrations), land cover change, and population distribution across the Netherlands. Using satellite-derived datasets from the Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service (CAMS), ESA WorldCover, and WorldPop, we processed and visualized environmental indicators to assess pollution patterns and their impact on population.

🌬

Air Quality Analysis

NO&dsub2; concentration mapping using CAMS reanalysis data at 0.1° resolution (~11km), classified according to EU air quality standards.

🏙

Land Cover Change

Detection of built area expansion between 2021 and 2023 using ESA WorldCover 10m annual land cover data.

👥

Population Exposure

Assessment of population living in different NO&dsub2; pollution classes using WorldPop population counts at ~92m resolution.

🌏

Interactive WebGIS

Web-based interactive map built with OpenLayers to visualize and explore all processing results.

Key Findings

NO&dsub2; Concentration

All 14 provinces of the Netherlands fall within Class 1 (≤10 μg/m³) or Class 2 (10-25 μg/m³) of EU air quality standards. No provinces exceed Class 2.

Population at Risk

90.2% of the Netherlands population lives in areas with Class 2 NO&dsub2; levels (10-25 μg/m³), highlighting widespread moderate pollution exposure.

Land Cover Change

Built area changes between 2021 and 2023 show ongoing urbanization patterns. The LCC map identifies areas of stable, gained, and lost built-up land.

NO&dsub2; Trend (2021-2023)

Annual Mean Absolute Change (AMAC) analysis reveals decreasing NO&dsub2; levels across most regions, consistent with European air quality trends.

Technologies Used

GDAL / QGIS

Geospatial data processing: reprojection, raster alignment, zonal statistics, and format conversions.

Python

Data analysis, raster processing with NumPy, statistical computations, and visualization with Matplotlib.

OpenLayers

Web-based interactive map with multiple basemaps, vector layers, and GIS controls.

GeoJSON / GPKG

Vector data formats for administrative boundaries and bivariate classification results.
